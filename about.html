What is SociAble?

Not just a chatbot. SociAble is a social practice sandbox for kids: pick a scenario (grocery store, playground, classroom), talk to a character, and see how your words affect their mood. If you're kind, they smile. If you're rude, they get hurt—so you learn cause and effect in a safe space. The "vibe check" gently flags mean language and suggests rephrasing instead of blocking. Goal: practice real social moments with real emotional feedback.

How can SociAble help an autistic child? Many autistic children find social situations unpredictable and overwhelming. SociAble is designed to support practice in ways that can reduce that stress: predictable scenarios (same places, clear goals) so there's less surprise; visual feedback (the character's face and mood) so the link between words and feelings is clear; no real-world consequences—mistakes only affect the character in the sandbox, so it's safe to try and rephrase; gentle "try again" instead of harsh correction when language is unkind; and clear goals per scenario so practice has structure. It's a tool to rehearse social moments at the child's pace, and works well alongside a parent, caregiver, or educator.
